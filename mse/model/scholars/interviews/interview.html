Scholar Interviews

Interview of Dr. Lisa Norling

Kathleen Neumann, interviewer

Interview Questions

Click a question below to play that segment of the video. Or click here to play the complete video.

  1. Changes in Women and Family from the 18th to 19th Century
    Can you explain how the role of women and the concept of family in America changed from the 18th to the 19th Century? (5:16)
  2. Marriage as a Civil Contract
    In 17th-century marriages in New England, there were not any religious aspects to the ceremony. Can you explain? (8:35)
  3. Revolution and Patricarchy
    Did the shift cause the Revolution or did the Revolution cause the shift? (2:09)
  4. Women Working at Sea
    If 18th-century women's roles were subordinate to men but also flexible, why wasn't work on the sea open to women? (1:29)
  5. Men Working at Sea
    Why are men the ones to go to sea? (1:38)
  6. Women in the Maritime World
    How did women see their work in relation to the maritime world? (1:55)
  7. Masculinity of Going to Sea
    Why was it so important for sea-faring to be seen as masculine? (3:01)
  8. Views of the Home
    In your book, you describe a shift in the 19th-Century American culture to a more romantic, sentimental and idealized view of the home as a safe-haven from the outside world. Where did this view come from? (3:55)
  9. Expectations of Women
    With this shift, were women expected to focus solely on domestic and family concerns? (1:56)
  10. Women and Freedom
    In general, do you think women of the 18th-Century enjoy more freedom than women of the 19th-Century? (2:56)
  11. Ideals of Commitment to Marriage
    In your book, you discuss how 19th-Century men and women cling to ideals of a deep commitment to marriage and romantic domesticism to cope with long separations. Is this specific to maritime cultures? (3:53)
  12. Living up to Ideals
    You discuss how those ideals don't really fit the reality of maritime couples. Why did men and women continue to cling to those ideas which were so difficult to achieve? (3:42)

About This Interview

Dr. Lisa Norling was interviewed by high school teacher Kathleen Neumann in July of 2012, after giving her lecture: "Gender Dynamics in American Maritime History" at the Frank C. Munson Institute of American Maritime Studies at Mystic Seaport. The lecture recordings and interview were made possible by the National Endowment for the Humanities.
